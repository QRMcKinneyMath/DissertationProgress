\begin{df}[\GateauxDerivative]
\label{def:GateauxDerivative}
\rm
Let $X$ be a \TVS.
Let $Y$ be a \Hausdorff \TVS.
Let $C \subset X$. 
Let $x_0 \in C$. 
Let $f:C \to X$. 
Suppose $\{x_0\} \times X$ is a subset of the \FunctionDomain
of $f_+'$. 
Furthermore, suppose there exists $T \in BL(X,Y)$ such that 
for each $y \in X$, 
\begin{equation*}
Ty = f_+'(x_0,y)
\end{equation*}
The we say that the \GateauxDerivative of $f$ exists at $x_0$, 
we write $T=f'(x_0)$, and we call 
$f'(x_0)$ the \GateauxDerivative of $f$ at $x_0$. 
Furthermore, we say that $f$ is \GateauxDifferentiable at $x_0$.

\end{df}
