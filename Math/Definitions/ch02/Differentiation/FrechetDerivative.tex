\begin{df}[\FrechetDerivative]
\label{def:FrechetDerivative}
\rm
Let $X$ be a \TVS.
Let $Y$ be a \Hausdorff \TVS. 
Let $C \subset X$. 
Let $x_0 \in C$. 
Let $f:C \to X$. 
Let $f$ be \GateauxDifferentiable at $x_0$.
Let $U$ be a \Neighborhood of $0$ in $X$. 
Futhermore, 
suppose that for each \Neighborhood  $V$ of $0$ in $Y$,
there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that 
whenever $\abs{t} < \delta$ and $y \in U$, 
\begin{equation*}
\frac{f(x_0+ty)-f(x_0)}{t} \in U+f'(x_0)y
\end{equation*}
Then we say that $f$ is 
\FrechetDifferentiable at $x_0$ 
and that $f'(x_0)$ is the 
\FrechetDerivative of $f$ at $x_0$. 
\end{df}
