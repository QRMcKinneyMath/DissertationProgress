\begin{df}[\AntiSymmetricRelation]
    Let $X \neq \emptyset$ be a set. 
    Let $R$ be a \Relation on X. 
    We say that $R$ is \SymmetricRelation, 
    or equivalently we say that
    $R$ posseses 
    \RelationSymmetry
    if whenever $(a,b) \in R$ and $(b,a) \in R$, we 
    must ahve $a = b$.
\end{df}
