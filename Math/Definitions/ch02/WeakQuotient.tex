\begin{prop}[Weak Quotients]
\label{prop:weakquotients}
\rm
Let $X$ be  a \TVS. 
Let $\{Y_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}$ be a collection of 
\TopologicalSpaces.
For each $\alpha \in A$, let 
$\phi_{\alpha}:X \to Y_\alpha$ be \ContinuousFunction.
Furthere, suppose that the collection $\{\phi_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}$
has the property that for each $x,y \in X$, 
If $y \in x+\overline{\{0\}}$, then
for each $\alpha \in A$, $\phi_\alpha(x) = \phi_\alpha(y)$. 
For each $\alpha \in A$, define $\tilde{\phi}_{\alpha}:X/\overline{\{0\}} \to Y_{\alpha}$ by 
setting, for $x \in X$,
$\tilde{\phi}_\alpha ([x]) = \phi_\alpha(x)$. 
Let $\scT_w$ denote the \WeakTopology on $X$ induced by $\{\phi_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}$. 
Let $\tilde{\scT}_w$ denote the \WeakTopology on $X/\overline{\{0\}}$ induced by 
$\{\tilde{\phi}_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}$. 
Suppose $\scT_w$ separates points in $X$ mod $\overline{\{0\}}$.
Then, 
\begin{equation}
(X,\T_w)/\overline{\{0\}} = (X/\overline{\{0\}}, \T_{\tilde{w}})
\end{equation}
\begin{proof}
Let $Q$ denote the \QuotientMap of $X$. 
Each $\tilde{\phi}_\alpha:X/\overline{\{0\}} \to Y_\alpha$ is 
\ContinuousFunction.
To see this, let $U \in \scT_{\alpha}$. 
Then since $\phi_\alpha$ is \ContinuousFunction, 
since $Q$ is \OpenFunction, and since 
$\phi_\alpha = \tilde{\phi}_\alpha \circ Q$, 
$\tilde{\phi}_\alpha^{-1}(U) = Q\pa{ \phi_\alpha^{-1}(U)}$ is \SetOpen in $X/\overline{\{0\}}$.
A basis for $(X, \scT_w)/\overline{\{0\}}$ is given by 
\begin{equation*}
\braces{Q \pa{\bigcap\limits_{i=1}^n \phi_{\alpha_i}^{-1}\pa{W_i}} : (n \in \mathbb{N}) (W_i \in \scT_{\alpha_i})(\{\alpha_i\}_{i=1}^n \subset A)}
\end{equation*}
Let $n \in \mathbb{N}$. 
Now let $\{\alpha_i\}_{i=1}^n \subset A$.
For each $i$ with $1 \leq i \leq n$, let $W_i \in \scT_{\alpha_i}$. 
Then, since each $\phi_{\alpha_i}^{-1}(W_{\alpha_i})$ is \SetOpen in $X$, 
they are all \Fibers relative to $Q$. 
Hence, we have 
\begin{align*}
Q \pa{ \bigcap\limits_{i=1}^n \phi_{\alpha_i}^{-1} \pa{W_{\alpha_i}}} & = \bigcap\limits_{i=1}^n Q  \pa{ \phi_{\alpha_i}^{-1} \pa{W_{\alpha_i}}} \\
& = \bigcap\limits_{i=1}^n \tilde{\phi}_{\alpha_i}^{-1} \pa{W_{\alpha_i}}\\
& \in \scT_{\tilde{w}}
\end{align*}
Hence the \Topology  on $(X, \scT_w)/\overline{0}$ is a subset of 
$\scT_{\tilde{w}}$.
The reverse construction works just the same, so that the two topologies are equal. 
\end{proof} 
\end{prop}
