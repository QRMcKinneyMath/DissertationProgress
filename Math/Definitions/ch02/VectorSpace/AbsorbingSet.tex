\begin{df}[\Absorbing]
\label{def:AbsorbingSet}
\rm
    Let V be a \VectorSpace
    over a 
    \Field
    $\F \in \{\R, \C\}$. 
    Let $A, B \subset V$. 
    We say that $A$ 
    \Absorbs
    $B$ if 
    there exists a 
    $c > 0$ 
    such that
    $B \subset cA$. 
    In such a scenario, 
    $A$ is also said to 
    \Absorb $B$, 
    and we say that $B$ is 
    \Absorbed by $A$.
    If $A$ \Absorbs every singleton in $V$, 
    then we call $A$ an 
    \AbsorbingSet or we say that $A$
    is \Absorbing. 
\end{df}
